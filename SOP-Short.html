Levels of Force

Level 1 - Officer Presence

No force is used.

Level 2 - Verbalization

Force is not physical.

Level 3 - Empty Hand Control

LEOs use bodily force to gain control of a situation.

Level 4 - Less-Lethal Methods

LEOs use less-lethal technologies to gain control of a situation.

Level 5 - Lethal Force

LEOs use lethal weapons to gain control of a situation.

Reasonable Suspicion vs Probable Cause

Reasonable Suspicion (RS)

A gut feeling based on facts that someone might be doing something illegal.

Allows: Stopping and briefly detaining someone (traffic stop, frisk)

Probable Cause (PC)

Reasonable belief, based on facts and evidence, that a crime has been or is being committed.

Allows: Making an arrest, searching a car/house/person, getting a warrant

Crime Classifications

Infraction

Minor offense, usually no jail time

Examples: Jaywalking, parking violations

Misdemeanor

Minor offense with possible jail time

Examples: Simple assault, petty theft

Felony

Serious crime with major consequences

Examples: Murder, armed robbery, GTA

Deadly Force Policy

Only use your firearm:

  • If your life or another's is in immediate danger
  • Give him 3 warnings to surrender
  • As a last resort, not a first reaction
  • Always justify the force used in a use-of-force report (if applicable)

Note: Unlawful shootings = demotion, strike, or removal from the department.

HUT Charge (Hold Until Trial)

A HUT charge is used when a suspect is arrested for a serious crime (usually a felony) and cannot be granted bail and must stay in custody until their court trial.

When to Apply HUT:

  • Suspects committed a violent felony (e.g., murder, terrorism)
  • Suspect is a flight risk or likely to tamper with evidence
  • The suspect has repeat offenses or pending cases
  • No judge or bail hearing is currently available (RP reason)
